Property Maintenance Policy

Effective: January 1, 2026

1. Emergency Maintenance

Emergency maintenance items require immediate response (within 2 hours):

  • Flooding or water damage
  • Gas leaks or gas smell
  • Fire or fire damage
  • No heat in winter (below 40°F)
  • Electrical hazards
  • Broken locks or security breaches
  • Sewage backup

Emergency vendor dispatch is authorized without owner approval.

2. Urgent Maintenance

Urgent items require response within 24 hours:

  • No hot water
  • Broken/malfunctioning HVAC
  • Refrigerator not working
  • Broken windows
  • Pest infestation

3. Standard Maintenance

Standard items require response within 72 hours:

  • Leaky faucets or minor plumbing
  • Appliance issues (non-critical)
  • Cosmetic damage
  • Door/cabinet repairs
  • Light fixtures

4. Owner Approval Thresholds

Under $500 Auto-approved, proceed immediately
$500 – $2,000 Require owner notification and approval before work begins
Over $2,000 Require owner approval + second quote from alternative vendor
Emergency repairs Owner notified after dispatch regardless of cost

6. Tenant Communication

  • Acknowledge all maintenance requests within 15 minutes
  • Provide status updates at every workflow stage
  • Follow up within 24 hours after repair completion
  • All communications via SMS unless tenant prefers email

7. Tenant Verification

After every completed repair:

  • Send follow-up message asking if issue is resolved
  • If resolved: close work order, send invoice to owner
  • If not resolved: re-dispatch vendor or escalate to PM
  • If no response within 48 hours: send one reminder, then close with note

8. Billing

  • All repair costs billed to property owner
  • Invoice sent within 24 hours of work completion
  • Include: vendor name, work description, parts, labor, total cost
  • Late fee waived for first-time issues per payment plan policy
