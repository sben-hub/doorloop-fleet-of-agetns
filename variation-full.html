Explanations · Building blocks
Agent builder incl. knowledge base Sahar
Where PMs create and configure agents. Includes templates (pre-built agent configurations per use case), natural language setup, and agent preview. The knowledge base lives here too — PMs upload their policies, SOPs, and property-specific rules as part of building their agent. The agent is configured based on those policies, and may generate workflows automatically from them. All agents share one infrastructure.
Workflow engine Karin
The execution layer that connects everything. Every agent action, escalation, communication, and resolution runs through a workflow. Workflows are retryable, traceable, and auditable. Key open question: are workflows PM-built (no-code canvas), AI-generated from the knowledge base, or invisible to the PM entirely?
Communication center Sahar
Agents communicate with all stakeholders — tenants, owners, vendors, prospects — across all channels: voice, SMS, email, and tenant portal. PM has full visibility into what agents are saying and to whom. For review and oversight, not for doing the work.
Command center Karin
The single screen where a PM manages their agent team. Live view of every active agent and thread. Escalation controls — approve, override, reassign — without leaving the page. Distinct from the audit log: the command center is real-time and action-oriented. North star: % of escalations resolved without leaving this screen.
Audit log Karin
Every decision — human and AI — logged, timestamped, and searchable. Generated by the workflow engine at every step. Historical and accountability-oriented, not real-time. Answers the question: what happened, when, and why?
Open questions · Key decisions
Question 1
Who builds workflows?
DoorLoop pre-builds them
Pre-configured per use case. PM turns them on. Simple, not flexible.
PM builds them
No-code drag-and-drop canvas. Maximum flexibility, requires PM effort.
AI generates from knowledge base
PM describes intent in the agent builder. AI generates the workflow automatically. Most differentiated — and ties agent builder directly to workflow engine.
Question 2
Do we allow PMs to edit workflows?
Yes — full editing canvas
PMs see and modify every step. Powerful but complex.
Yes — guided editing only
PMs can tweak parameters but not structure. Safer UX.
No — workflows are invisible
AI runs workflows in the background. PM only sees outcomes and escalations in the command center.
Question 3
Are all agents conversational?
Yes — all agents communicate
Every agent has a comms layer with tenants, owners, or vendors.
No — some agents are silent
Some agents act in the background (e.g. delinquency tracking, renewals) without outbound comms.
PM decides per agent
Communication is a configurable toggle at the agent level inside the agent builder.
Building block deep-dive · Command Center Karin
What it is

The Command Center is the single screen where a property manager oversees their entire AI agent team in real time. It shows every active thread, every pending escalation, and every completed action — organized by urgency.

Think of it as air traffic control for property management AI. The PM doesn't do the work — the agents do. The PM approves, overrides, and redirects from one screen. The north-star metric: % of escalations resolved without leaving this page.

A PM's morning with the Command Center
1.Sarah opens DoorLoop and lands on the Command Center. She sees 2 items flagged "Act Now" in red, 1 item to "Review" in yellow, and 2 completed items in green.
2.The top red item is an emergency maintenance request: a water heater failure. The Maintenance Agent already dispatched a vendor and got a $2,800 quote. Sarah taps "Approve & send to owner" without leaving the page.
3.The second red item is a repeat late payer. The Rent Collection Agent recommends a formal notice. Sarah reviews the history and approves. Done in 30 seconds.
4.By 9:15am, Sarah has resolved all escalations. Her agents continue working autonomously. She checks back at lunch — two new green items. No new reds. This is the vision.

How competitors handle this
EliseAI
Centralizes communications into one hub but focuses on conversation management, not agent oversight. PMs still manage individual threads rather than an agent team. No unified escalation view.
Wonderful.ai
Their enterprise "Monitor" product provides a centralized inbox for AI conversations. Closer to our vision, but still conversation-centric rather than action-centric. No prioritized escalation feed.
Aptly
Aptly's Dashboard shows a Team Performance Dashboard that tracks metrics and response times. It's analytics-oriented, not action-oriented. PMs review performance after the fact, not manage in real time.
No competitor today gives a PM a single screen to manage an agent team with prioritized escalations, inline approvals, and full audit trail. This is the gap.

Audit Log
Historical, accountability-oriented. What happened, when, and why.
What it is

The Audit Log is the complete, searchable record of every decision made in your property management operation — whether by an AI agent or a human. Every workflow step, every escalation, every approval, every communication is logged with a timestamp and full context.

It's generated automatically by the workflow engine. PMs don't create audit entries — the system does. This is how you answer: "What happened with unit 3A last Tuesday?" or "Show me every decision the maintenance agent made this month."

When the audit log matters
"Why was this vendor chosen for the repair?"
The audit log shows the AI evaluated 3 vendors, selected ABC Plumbing based on rating (4.8), availability (same-day), and cost ($2,800 vs $3,200 average). Decision logged at 9:42am.
"Who approved the lease renewal at that rate?"
The log shows the AI agent proposed a 3% increase based on market comps. Sarah Chen approved at 2:15pm on Jan 12. The tenant signed digitally at 4:30pm.
"Show me everything that happened with this tenant's complaint."
Full timeline: tenant submitted request (8:00am), AI triaged as urgent (8:01am), vendor dispatched (8:15am), tenant notified (8:16am), vendor arrived (10:30am), resolution confirmed (2:00pm). Every step logged.
